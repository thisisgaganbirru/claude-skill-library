# Platform Playbooks — Deep Dive Reference

## Instagram (2024–2025 Algorithm & Strategy)

### How the Algorithm Works
Instagram's algorithm in 2024 has two distinct systems running in parallel: a **follower graph** (content shown to people who already follow you) and an **interest graph** (content shown to people who don't follow you, primarily via Reels and Explore). Growing on Instagram today means optimizing for both simultaneously.

The key signals Instagram weighs, in rough order of importance:
1. **Watch time / completion rate** (especially for Reels)
2. **Saves** — the highest-intent signal; signals "I want to come back to this"
3. **Shares** — signals the content is worth spreading; drives reach to non-followers
4. **Comments** — especially back-and-forth threads, which signal community
5. **Likes** — still matter but are the lowest-weight engagement signal

### Reels Strategy (Growth Engine)
Reels is the primary discoverability mechanism on Instagram. Every Reel should be conceived as content for someone who has never heard of you.

**The Reels formula that consistently performs:**
- Hook (0–1s): visual pattern interrupt or bold verbal statement
- Tease (1–3s): establish the payoff the viewer will get if they keep watching
- Value delivery (3–25s): the meat; fast-paced, no filler
- Soft CTA (final 2s): "save this" or "follow for more [specific thing]"

**Technical specs that matter:**
- Upload aspect ratio: 9:16, 1080×1920
- Keep text overlays out of the bottom 20% (hidden by UI elements)
- Use closed captions — 85% of Reels are watched without sound
- Audio: trending audio gives an algorithmic boost; use niche-relevant trending sounds, not random ones

**Hashtag strategy 2024:** Instagram has deprioritized hashtags as a discovery mechanism since 2023. Use 3–5 highly specific, niche-relevant hashtags rather than a wall of 30. They still have minor discovery value but the keyword in your caption is now more important.

### Stories Strategy (Loyalty Loop)
Stories are shown primarily to existing followers. They are your relationship-deepening tool, not your growth tool. Use them to:
- Show the human behind the brand (process, daily life, opinions)
- Run polls, quizzes, and questions to drive interaction (algorithm rewards)
- Promote new posts with a "tap to see" or swipe-up link
- Use the Close Friends feature for premium or vulnerable content that strengthens loyalty among your core audience

### Profile Optimization
A profile visitor decides whether to follow in under 5 seconds. Your profile must immediately communicate:
1. **Who this is for** (the target audience)
2. **What they'll get** (the value proposition)
3. **Why you** (the differentiating angle or credibility signal)

Bio formula: [Who you help] + [what transformation/value you provide] + [one personality/human detail] + [CTA with link]. Example: "Helping South Asian women invest their first $1K 📈 | CA turned money nerd | Free starter guide 👇"

## YouTube (2024–2025 Algorithm & Strategy)

### How the Algorithm Works
YouTube is the world's second-largest search engine and a recommendation engine simultaneously. The algorithm optimizes for **viewer satisfaction** (measured by watch time, likes, survey responses, and return views) not just click volume.

The two main discovery paths:
- **Search**: User types a query; SEO-optimized titles, descriptions, and video content (spoken keywords are transcribed) determine ranking
- **Suggested / Recommended**: YouTube shows your video alongside or after similar content; driven by watch time, topic clustering, and engagement signals

### The Click-Retention-Satisfaction Loop
Every YouTube video's performance is evaluated on three sequential metrics:
1. **CTR (Click-Through Rate)**: % of impressions that became clicks. Anything above 4–7% is good; above 10% is exceptional. Optimized by thumbnail + title.
2. **Average View Duration / % Viewed**: How much of the video the average viewer watches. Above 40% is healthy; above 60% is strong.
3. **Satisfaction Signals**: Likes, comments, survey responses, and whether viewers clicked another video immediately after.

YouTube pushes videos that pass all three gates. A video with high CTR but low retention is punished harder than one with moderate CTR and high retention.

### Title & Thumbnail (The Ad for Your Video)
The title-thumbnail combination is your video's advertisement. They must work together — the thumbnail creates curiosity, the title resolves the curiosity *just enough* to compel the click.

**Title principles:**
- Front-load the primary keyword (first 60 characters visible in search results)
- Create a curiosity gap or make a bold promise
- Use numbers, contrasts, and emotional triggers (e.g., "I tried X for 30 days — here's what actually happened")
- Avoid clickbait that over-promises; this tanks retention and satisfaction

**Thumbnail principles:**
- Use a face with strong, readable emotion (human faces draw the eye)
- Bold, readable text at large sizes (test at thumbnail size — if you can't read it, neither can viewers)
- High contrast — your thumbnail must stand out in a sea of thumbnails
- Consistent brand style (recurring color palette, font, composition style = recognizable brand)

### Video Structure for Retention
The first 30 seconds determine whether your viewer stays for the rest. Use the **False Open**: start with the most compelling moment or a bold preview of the payoff *before* the intro. Delay the logo bumper or "welcome back" for the 30-second mark.

Mid-video retention tactics: use **pattern interrupts** (B-roll cut, graphic, music change) every 60–90 seconds to reset attention. Tease what's coming next just before each transition ("and this brings me to the part most people completely miss...").

### SEO Deep-Dive for YouTube
- **Title**: Primary keyword, front-loaded, under 70 characters
- **Description**: First 200 characters are shown in search results without expansion — make them keyword-rich and compelling. Include secondary keywords naturally throughout.
- **Chapters**: Timestamps with keyword-rich labels. They appear as chapters in search results and help YouTube understand what your video covers.
- **Tags**: Secondary keywords and long-tail variations. Less important than they used to be, but still worth doing.
- **Cards and End Screens**: Always use them to drive viewers to related content; this builds topic authority and extends session time (a strong satisfaction signal).
- **Pinned Comment**: Pin a comment asking a question related to the video topic. This increases comment count, which is a ranking signal.

### Shorts Strategy (Funnel, Not Main Channel)
YouTube Shorts get their own discovery system but do not substantially grow a long-form channel's subscribers directly. Use Shorts as a **top-of-funnel awareness tool** — repurpose key moments from long-form videos, direct viewers to the full video, and build awareness with audiences who might not find you through search.

## TikTok (2024–2025 Algorithm & Strategy)

### How the Algorithm Works
TikTok's For You Page (FYP) is the purest interest graph in social media. It cares almost exclusively about **content signals** (what the video is about) and **engagement signals** (watch time, replays, shares, comments, likes). Follower count is nearly irrelevant. This means TikTok is simultaneously the most democratic platform (anyone can go viral) and the most volatile (you can lose distribution overnight if a video underperforms).

Key TikTok signals in order of importance:
1. **Completion rate + replays** — the #1 signal; a 100% completion rate is the strongest possible signal
2. **Shares** — especially off-platform shares (someone sent this to a friend = highly relevant)
3. **Comments** — especially questions and debates
4. **Likes** — least weighted but still meaningful
5. **"Not Interested" reports** — actively negative signal; reduces distribution

### The TikTok Hook Formula
TikTok scrolling is faster than any other platform. The first frame and first spoken word must instantly signal relevance to the target viewer.

Best-performing hook types:
- **Controversy hook**: "This is the worst advice in [niche] — and everyone's giving it"
- **Relatability hook**: Start mid-scene in a situation your audience recognizes ("So I'm at the gym and my trainer tells me...")
- **Curiosity gap hook**: "I didn't believe this until it happened to me"
- **Value hook**: "3 things your [job/hobby/identity] should know about [thing] but probably doesn't"

The hook exists in the **first 1 second**. Unlike YouTube where you have 30 seconds, TikTok users swipe within 1–2 seconds of content that doesn't immediately resonate.

### Sound Strategy
Trending sounds provide an algorithmic boost *when used correctly*. Mismatched audio (a sad trending sound on a hype video, or a funny sound on a serious topic) confuses the algorithm and reduces the FYP push. Use trending sounds that match your content's emotional register, or use original audio to build sound recognition (your own recurring intro music or catchphrase can become your brand on TikTok).

### Comment Engagement
TikTok weights creator-to-commenter replies heavily. Reply to as many comments as possible in the first hour after posting. TikTok's "reply with video" feature (respond to a comment with a new video) is an excellent retention loop — it creates a canonical content series out of Q&A and drives reply views.

## LinkedIn (Creator Mode Strategy)

### Algorithm Mechanics
LinkedIn's algorithm in 2024 rewards content that drives genuine **professional conversation**. The key signal is comment quality and volume, not likes. A post with 10 substantive comments outperforms a post with 200 likes and 3 comments.

The algorithm has a "golden hour" — the first 1–2 hours after posting determine whether LinkedIn amplifies the post. During this window, seed the conversation: reply to every comment, ask follow-up questions, and post your own comment with an additional resource or question.

### Content That Works on LinkedIn
- **Personal professional stories**: "I got rejected by 40 companies before getting this offer. Here's what changed." These outperform thought leadership pieces 3:1 in engagement.
- **Contrarian takes**: Challenge conventional wisdom in your industry with a data-backed or experience-backed perspective.
- **Lessons from failure**: LinkedIn audiences, unlike Instagram, respond extremely well to vulnerability that is paired with insight.
- **Data-driven insights**: Share a stat or research finding with your original interpretation.

### Format Notes
- Native documents (PDFs uploaded as carousels) are LinkedIn's highest-performing format for educational content
- Native video gets priority in the feed over YouTube links — always upload video directly
- Text-only posts can still go viral if the writing is strong — LinkedIn does not require visuals

## Pinterest (Visual Search Engine Strategy)

### The Fundamental Difference
Pinterest is not a social network — it is a **visual search engine**. Pins have a 6-month content lifespan (vs. hours on TikTok or days on Instagram). This makes Pinterest the best platform for **evergreen, SEO-driven content** — content that answers a recurring question or desire.

Best niches on Pinterest: food/recipes, home décor, fashion, DIY/crafts, travel itineraries, wedding planning, personal finance infographics, printables/digital downloads.

### Pinterest SEO
Pinterest boards, pin titles, pin descriptions, and alt text are all indexed by **Pinterest search and Google search**. Treat every pin description like a blog meta description: 150–300 words, keyword-rich, naturally written.

Board names matter: "Dinner Recipes" is weaker than "Easy Weeknight Dinner Recipes for Families." The specificity signals topical relevance.

Ideal pinning frequency: 5–10 pins per day (mix of original content and repins). Pinterest rewards high-volume, consistent activity more than any other platform.

### Monetization on Pinterest
Pinterest traffic converts well to email list signups, blog pageviews, and product sales. Add UTM parameters to all link destinations to track which pins drive the most traffic and revenue.

## Emerging Platforms (2024–2025)

### Threads (Meta)
Threads is Twitter/X's main competitor, built on the Instagram social graph. Currently in growth phase, meaning organic reach is very high. Best used for conversational, opinion-based content. Cross-posting strategies between Instagram and Threads are straightforward given shared account infrastructure.

### YouTube Podcasts
YouTube has leaned heavily into podcasting as a content format. Long-form conversation videos with static or minimal visuals are getting recommended alongside traditional YouTube content. If a creator has podcast-style content, native YouTube upload (not just audio-only platforms) is increasingly important.

### Beehiiv / Substack (Newsletter Platforms)
Email newsletters are the highest-ownership distribution channel — no algorithm, direct audience relationship. For creators building toward monetization, growing an email list should be treated as the goal, with social media as the funnel. Beehiiv and Substack both have built-in discovery networks for subscriber growth.
